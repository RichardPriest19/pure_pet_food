Hitting the Ground Running

The first 90 days
at Pure Pet Food.

Before day one, Richard would already be thinking about this.

No changes, no declarations. Deep immersion in the technology estate, the team, and the business.
01
Full technology audit Map every system, integration, dependency, and risk across the subscription platform, ERP, website, and internal tools. Understand what's custom, what's fragile, and what's load-bearing.
02
Meet every stakeholder 1-to-1s with the Head of Development, engineering team, and every functional leader — marketing, operations, fulfilment, finance, customer experience — technology touches all of them.
03
Cybersecurity baseline Rapid assessment of the current security posture. Identify the highest-risk gaps before anything else — especially relevant given the pace of growth and the highly custom estate.
04
Understand the backlog Get into the actual development backlog. Understand what's been deferred, what's accumulating as technical debt, and where the engineering team is under the most pressure.
Form views. Begin structuring the approach. Align with founders on what matters most.
01
Build vs buy assessment A clear-eyed view of the custom estate. Where continued in-house development is right vs where a proven SaaS solution reduces risk, cost, and maintenance burden — particularly across ERP and operational systems.
02
Architecture and scalability review Assess the platform's ability to handle the next phase of growth — both domestic and European expansion. Identify structural changes needed before they become emergencies.
03
Align with founders on priorities Present an honest picture of where the technology is strong, where it's at risk, and what needs to happen in what order. Shared understanding before committing to a roadmap.
04
Team capability mapping Understand the development team individually — strengths, gaps, morale, career ambitions. Identify where the team needs support, development, or additional resource.
A clear technology strategy on the table. Early wins in place. The team moving with direction.
01
Technology roadmap — 12-month view A prioritised, board-ready roadmap balancing platform stability, security improvements, technical debt reduction, and growth-enabling features. Sequenced and resourced realistically.
02
Security remediation plan A time-bound plan to close the most critical security gaps — with clear ownership, milestones, and communication to leadership. No more unknown unknowns.
03
Early wins delivered At least one tangible, visible improvement already in place — whether a process change, a security fix, or a structural team improvement — to demonstrate pace alongside planning.
04
AI opportunity map An initial view of where AI and automation can accelerate Pure Pet Food's operations — from subscription management and personalisation to internal workflow efficiency. Practical, not theoretical.
